Sprint Headline — Refills shipped end-to-end, exclusive-contracts work began, and search got dramatically faster

Milestone 21 closed at 95% (65 of 68 milestone items) — the strongest completion in the recent run (M18 82%, M19 76%) and only 3 items carrying to M22. The fortnight delivered squarely on its three-part brief. Refills moved from workstream to shipped product: complete-order + NetSuite invoice generation (#4919), refill volume now counts toward contract progress (#4922), closed-period-safe invoice dating (#5098), and a full bulk CSV/XLSX import path — backend (#5111) and validating UI (#5112). The "exclusive contracts for finance" workstream started and largely landed — the Extend Contract flag + UI (#5172) and Venue Collections / VENUE_PICKUP SKU work (#5197, #5201) — teeing up M22's "test the exclusive-contracts feature" goal. And a sizeable unplanned win landed alongside: the Global Search Performance epic (#5091, 6/6) replaced slow LIKE scans with FULLTEXT MATCH + resilient fan-out across orders, organisations, locations and assets.

Watch item — "Exclusive contracts" spans two overlapping epics, and a large perf epic arrived unplanned

Two process signals worth a minute at retro. First, the contracts goal is split across two epics — #4903 Extend Contract Option (flag-based end-date handling + Slack notifier) and #5173 Venue Collections (VENUE_PICKUP SKUs + pricing) — with related work also touching Refills SKU/pricing. Before M22 testing begins, it's worth confirming which epic owns "the exclusive-contracts feature" and how the two interlock. Second, the Global Search Performance epic was substantial unplanned scope that the team chose to absorb mid-sprint. The outcome is excellent, but it's a scope-freeze data point: was it a deliberate priority call, and did it displace anything? Both are healthy questions for a 95% sprint, not problems.

What worked

  • Refills landed end-to-end. A multi-sprint workstream became live product — orders, invoicing, bulk import and reporting all shipped together.
  • 95% completion, 3 carry-over. The cleanest close in the recent run — the hygiene runbook is holding.

What to review

  • Two epics for one goal. Extend Contract (#4903) and Venue Collections (#5173) both serve "exclusive contracts." Clarify ownership and boundaries before M22 testing.
  • Unplanned scope. The search-perf epic was a big mid-sprint add. Great outcome — but was it a deliberate priority call, and did the Day-8 freeze hold?
  • Refills now live. With real NS invoices generating, we need eyes on production — mis-labeling (#5130) was caught late.
  • NS hardening (#4853) still open. The tail keeps closing but the epic won't die — decide what "done" looks like.

Open questions for M22

  • M22 is broad. The brief lists six themes (contracts testing, Booths API, DEV-OC/NS mock, AWS cost, Playwright migration, LaunchDarkly). Which three are the real priorities?
  • Who tests exclusive contracts? Confirm an owner and a definition of "tested" for the M22 goal.
  • Booths API integration is net-new — does it need a discovery spike before commitment?
  • RainForest → Playwright and LaunchDarkly work are enablers, not features — reserve explicit capacity or they slip.
