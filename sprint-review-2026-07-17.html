Sprint Headline — First 100% close of the run: 30 of 30, zero carry-over, on a deliberately right-sized scope

Milestone 22 closed at 100% (30 of 30 milestone items) — the first perfect close since Rob took the board — after M21's sprawling 98-item fortnight. The brief asked for “a single major EPIC, a lot smaller than the last, plus tech-debt housekeeping,” and the smaller-and-finish-it part worked exactly as intended. The centre of gravity became the Dev-OC feedback bridge (epic #5422): a staging feedback widget that turns in-app feedback into GitHub issues and Slack DMs (#5421/#5427), then matured across the fortnight — dedicated bot token (#5439), dedupe hardening (#5440), public-bucket screenshots (#5464), viewport-only capture + preview (#5468), a shared annotation canvas with region highlight (#5477), and the Keggy mascot button (#5478, #5483). Around it: a financial-correctness fix to contract-brewer freight shadow pricing (#5444/#5446), a venue-collections polish tail, solid BAU reliability fixes, and a real CI/DX modernisation push (Blacksmith runners #5479, PR notifier v2 #5486, preflight guard #5222). Release v1.89.27 is drafted and in review today (21 PRs).

Watch item — the brief and the board told different stories, and two named goals produced no tracked work

The board brief named the “Super Cool Fast Keg Scanning Tool” as the sprint's single major EPIC and “start work on the Booths API integration” — neither appears anywhere in the milestone's 30 closed items. The feedback-bridge epic (#5422, opened July 9, mid-sprint) absorbed the sprint instead — a good outcome on its own terms, but it displaced the stated plan without the brief ever being updated. Booths API has now produced zero tracked work across three consecutive sprint briefs (M20-report, M21-report, this one) and is absent from the M23 brief entirely — that needs an explicit decision: deliberately parked, or lost? Similarly, M23's brief says “completing the Symfony 8 upgrade” — but no Symfony-8 work is visible in this milestone, so confirm where that upgrade actually stands and get it tracked on the board before the sprint builds on it.
