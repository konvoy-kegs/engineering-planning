Unplanned mid-sprint pull — NetSuite atomicity bug (#4852)

A P0 NetSuite invoice atomicity bug (KONVOY-API-2T6 / 2VA) was discovered mid-sprint: order completion was failing when NS rejected an invoice, leaving Katch Cloud in an inconsistent state. The fix shipped (PR #4858) plus three downstream issues this week (#4936 silent failure path, #4937 NZ state mapping, #4933 Casual exclusive rental pricing), and the broader hardening was scoped into epic #4853 with 10 follow-ups. This is the conversation for retro: was this discoverable earlier with better integration testing?

Retrospective — Team Themes

Three columns: what worked, what to review, and open questions for the M19 kickoff. Team-level only.

What worked

  • Review queue discipline. Held at 2 items the whole sprint — Friday review blocks and Bea reviewing some frontend PRs both contributed.
  • Feature-flag shipping for Fuel Levy. LD-gated rollout means we can stage exposure to brewery customers without a code change.
  • Bea ramped meaningfully. Five PRs merged (#4850, #4860, #4868, #4878, #4881) plus took the lead on the Orders feedback round.
  • Tooling investment compounded. prod-db-edit/access, SuiteQL CLI, and the Report-a-bug widget will all pay back for the rest of the quarter.
  • NS atomicity bug response. Triaged, fixed, and follow-up epic spun up inside the same sprint — that's healthy.

What to review

  • Could #4852 have been caught earlier? The atomicity bug shipped to prod undetected. Worth asking: do we need an integration test gate for NS-touching changes?
  • Refills barely moved. Goal 3 was "start Refills" — only Discovery is open. Was the goal realistic given concurrent FL + Rentals + the unplanned NS work?
  • Project-board Status drift. #4852 is closed on the milestone but still in Review on the board. The sprint hygiene runbook needs to catch this.
  • Volume of unplanned work. 11 items pulled in mid-sprint (mostly NS cascade). Reasonable for the cause, but worth quantifying against planned scope.

Open questions for M19

  • Refills ownership. Should Millicent own NS-1.x end-to-end while Rob drives the #4853 hardening epic?
  • Batch the completedDate trio? #4787, #4788, #4789 are tightly coupled — treat as one work unit and timebox?
