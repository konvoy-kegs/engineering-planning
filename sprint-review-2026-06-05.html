Sprint Headline — NS hardening matured, Refills moved from talk to code, Orders API got faster

Milestone 19 closed at 76% (45 of 59 milestone items) — 22 issues and 23 merged PRs — landing roughly in line with M18's 82%. The fortnight delivered on its stated brief ("finishing minor Rentals items, starting Refills discovery and work") and then some: the NS invoice-hardening epic (#4853) graduated from cascade fixes to its central architectural change — decoupling Katch Cloud writes from NetSuite sync (#5028); Refills became a real workstream with a fresh epic (#4912, replacing the stale #4308) and two backend/FE tickets shipped; and a small but high-leverage Orders-API performance push short-circuited order-number search onto an index range scan. The trade-off: 14 items carry to M20, and the board-wide Review column has drifted back up to 18 — the clearest hygiene regression to flag this sprint.

Watch item — Review column has drifted to 18 board-wide (M18 held it at 2)

The single biggest process signal this fortnight isn't in the milestone numbers — it's on the board. The Review column now holds 18 items, back to the Sprint 3 problem range and a long way from the disciplined 2 we sustained through M18. Several are stale older items (NS invoice endpoints #4255/#4261/#4268, infra PRs in kubernetes/terraform) that look parked rather than actively reviewed. With Rob still the primary reviewer, this is the first thing the sprint-hygiene runbook should sweep on Monday. Retro question: is Review accumulating because of reviewer bandwidth, or because items are landing there "done-ish" and not being closed out?

What We Shipped — Themes

Grouped by theme, not by individual. The fortnight broke into five streams: NetSuite reliability hardening (now the central decoupling work), Orders/Rentals polish & bug-fixing, an Orders-API performance push, Refills kick-off, and continued DX/tooling investment.

Theme 1 — NetSuite reliability: from cascade fixes to decoupling

The #4853 hardening epic matured past the M18 cascade fixes into its architectural core: Katch Cloud writes are now decoupled from NetSuite sync, with finance alerted on NS failure (#5028) — the structural fix the M18 atomicity bug was pointing at. Alongside it, error-handling hygiene landed: leaky 409 diagnostics separated from user-facing messages (#5030), ConflictException now returns a clean 409 instead of a 500 + Sentry noise (#4996), plus two SuiteQL/subsidiary hotfixes (#4967, #4956).

Theme 3 — Orders-API performance push

A small, compounding win on the Orders list API. Order-number search was short-circuited onto a unique-index range scan (#4982) and the optimisation was then ported into the raw-SQL path (#4984), with a COUNT-path correctness fix in optionalJoinStrings() alongside (#4980). This is the kind of latency work that pays back every time the Orders table loads.

On DX: a sentry-issue-triage skill (#4998), a SonarCloud-on-Dependabot CI unblock (#5015), and a fix so cd-dev-fe registers GitHub deployments (#4964).

What worked

What to review

  • Epic proliferation. #4912 and #4953 both spun up this sprint. Healthy or a sign of unfocused WIP?

Open questions for M20
