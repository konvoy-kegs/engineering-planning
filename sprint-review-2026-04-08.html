Sprint 2 Retrospective

What Went Well

High throughput despite transition. 47 items completed with a developer departing mid-sprint. Doug shipped 15+ PRs across releases v1.89.8 and v1.89.9 while running handover sessions.
Orders experience delivered. Full table redesign, year-over-year charts, org view, mobile fullscreen modals, and side panels all landed.
NetSuite billing tooling. Billing email audit command (#4478) and null email fix (#4477) addressed a real compliance risk identified during the sprint.
Infrastructure progress. Grafana Cloud alerts (#435), OpenTelemetry Phase 1 (#4268), and slow API query optimisation (#4457) all moved to review/completion.

What To Review

Review bottleneck hit critical levels. Review queue peaked at 14 items mid-sprint (3x normal). With only Rob as primary reviewer, this created a multi-day backlog. Need a process fix.
Planned vs actual disconnect. 24 SP were planned at sprint start, but 80+ SP of unplanned work was pulled in (Fuel Levy, NS Audit, security items). Scope management needs tightening.
Sprint milestone tracking. Only 8% complete at mid-sprint despite active work — items were tracked on the board but not properly milestoned. CEO visibility suffered.
Knowledge transfer compressed. Doug's handover happened in final days. Bea has recordings but limited hands-on pairing time.

Process Questions

Review process: Should we cap the Review column at 5 items? Introduce Friday review blitz blocks?
Scope freeze: Should we freeze scope after Day 8 of each sprint? The Fuel Levy and NS Audit both landed mid-sprint and derailed the plan.
Milestone discipline: Every sprint item should have a milestone from Day 1. Are we using GitHub milestones effectively?
Bea's onboarding: What's the best way to ramp her into Doug's codebase? Paired PRs? Smaller tickets first?
